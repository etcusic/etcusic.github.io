I am a full stack web developer with a background in logistics and staff management.


I am very fortunate to have had a broad array of opportunities and experiences in my life. After graduating from University with a degree in Philosophy I pursued a path towards becoming a chef. I worked my way through the ranks of restaurant kitchens and learned what it takes not only to create great dishes, but also how to lead a staff of workers and how to manage the logistics of a restaurant kitchen. As much as I still love to cook, I ultimately decided that the life of a chef wasn’t what I wanted.


My next step was travelling to Thailand where I worked with the Free Burma Rangers - a small NGO dedicated to providing relief to the hill tribes of Burma from the ongoing civil war. I knew I loved to travel, but I also have a deep-seated need to be a productive worker, so I figured travelling abroad and working towards a good cause was the right move. I settled into a role managing the logistics for the organization, coordinating with field team leaders and ensuring supplies were procured and transported to their required locations.


As logistics coordinator I developed a real passion for creating systems of efficiency. I even developed a simple supply tracking system that our team used to maintain better continuity within the department. I knew that I was only scratching the surface of my talent in this area and started thinking that a career in programming applications would be a good fit.


Since then I have completed the Flatiron Software Engineering Program, where I learned the basics of creating and managing full stack web applications. Discovering my talent in creating efficient applications has been exciting, and I’m eager to apply these skills in a team setting. To me there is nothing more beautiful than a group of people putting their skills and talents to achieve common goals, and I am looking forward to finding the right team in which to immerse myself.
